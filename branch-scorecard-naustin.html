How We Grade a Branch

Final grade = composite + growth modifier. The composite measures where you landed; the growth modifier measures where you're going.

Composite · 7 Metrics

Financial Engine (TUR + Sales Target) · Critical Numbers (Q1: Driver Safety + Personnel-Budget — 30% combined, the quarterly theme spine) · People + Customer (NPS + eNPS + Recognition). Each metric counted exactly once. Σ(weight × grade) / 5.

Growth Modifier · Revenue YoY

Breakout (≥+20%) +10 · Strong (+10-20%) +7 · Stable (+3-10%) +4 · Flat (0-3%) 0 · Slight decline (−3-0%) −3 · Declining (<−3%) −7. Captures trajectory without restructuring the composite.

Final Grade · Composite + Modifier

Final score = composite + growth. Breakout YoY can lift a branch by a full letter; declining can drop one. Cover shows both halves so the BM can read landing AND trajectory at once.

How the composite math works

Each metric has a weight (the seven weights sum to 100) and a grade worth points: Super Green = 5, Green = 4, Watch = 2, Red = 1.

SG = 5 G = 4 W = 2 R = 1

For each metric we multiply weight × grade points, then sum across all seven. A perfect Super Green sweep = 100 × 5 = 500, the maximum possible. We divide by 5 to bring that back down to a familiar 0–100 scale.

Composite = Σ(weight × grade points) ÷ 5

N. Austin worked example: TUR (20×5) + Sales (10×5) + NPS (15×5) + eNPS (15×4) + Recog (10×2) + DSS (15×2) + Personnel-Budget (15×4) = 395. Then 395 ÷ 5 = 79 (B composite). Add the +4 growth modifier → 83 (B+ final).

Why SG=5, G=4, W=2, R=1 and not 5/4/3/2/1? The skip from G to W creates a real penalty for Watch — a Watch metric isn't "halfway between Green and Red," it's noticeably off pace. An all-Green branch lands at 80 (B+ baseline). All-Watch lands at 40. All-Red lands at 20. Hitting plan should read as above-average; this scale enforces that.

We grow by growing our people and customers — not at their expense. Critical Numbers carry 30% combined weight in the composite — the quarterly theme isn't a footnote, it's a structural anchor. Growth lives alongside as a separate signal so a strong-trajectory branch in a tough quarter (or a declining branch propped up by past plan) is read honestly. NOI/Truck sits in the Financial Engine top block as ungraded reference this quarter (actively calibrating; see methodology details in the QGR packet). Strategic Weight tier on the cover hero is a parallel display lens — measures cash contribution scale (NOI share / revenue share / truck share), separate from the operational composite.

📍 How the grade is built
Branch grade = a 7-metric weighted composite plus a single growth-modifier adjustment. Each metric counts exactly once. Critical Numbers (Q1: Driver Safety + Personnel-Budget) preserved at 30% combined weight — the quarterly theme spine.

📍 Composite Math
SG=5, G=4, W=2 (Watch), R=1. Score = (Σ weight × grade) / 5. N. Austin composite = (20·5 TUR + 10·5 Sales + 15·5 NPS + 15·4 eNPS + 10·2 Recog + 15·2 DSS + 15·4 Personnel) / 5 = 395 / 5 = 79. Anchor points: all-Green = 80 (B+ baseline), all-Super Green = 100 (A+), all-Red = 20 (F floor).
📍 Growth Modifier Rubric
Breakout (≥+20%) +10 · Strong (+10-20%) +7 · Stable growth (+3-10%) +4 · Flat (0-3%) 0 · Slight decline (−3-0%) −3 · Declining (<−3%) −7. N. Austin's +5.3% YoY → Stable growth → +4. Final 79 + 4 = 83 → B+.

95+ A+ · 90+ A · 85+ A− · 80+ B+ · 75+ B · 70+ B− · 60+ C · 50+ D · <50 F.
