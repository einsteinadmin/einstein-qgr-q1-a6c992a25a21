Why this matters

NPS measures how likely customers are to recommend Einstein after their move. It's calculated from post-move surveys: % of promoters minus % of detractors. NPS is the strongest leading indicator of revenue growth — high-NPS branches see more repeat bookings, more referrals, and better online reviews. Service quality on every move is what moves this number.

Above the +60 SG floor. Anne's surveys this quarter are how leadership knows what's working — fill them out honestly.
Why this matters

eNPS measures how likely movers are to recommend Einstein as a place to work. It's the employee equivalent of customer NPS. eNPS is one of the clearest signals of team health and tracks closely with retention. Honest survey responses make the score useful — leadership uses it to know what's working at each branch.

Why this matters

Driver Safety Score combines a driver's Samsara harsh-event data with the % of drivers in the high-risk band. Safer driving lowers fleet insurance premiums, reduces accident-related downtime, and prevents claims. Drivers are the line of defense — every habit shows up here.

Why this matters

TUR (Truck Utilization Rate) is the % of available truck-hours that actually turned into booked moves. High TUR means trucks are earning revenue; low TUR means trucks sitting idle (cost without income). Drivers, dispatch, and sales all contribute. The bar chart shows where every branch landed.

How We Grade a Branch

Final grade = composite + growth modifier. The composite measures where you landed; the growth modifier measures where you're going.

Composite · 7 Metrics

Financial Engine (TUR + Sales Target) · Critical Numbers (Q1: Driver Safety + Personnel-Budget — 30% combined, the quarterly theme spine) · People + Customer (NPS + eNPS + Recognition). Each metric counted exactly once. Σ(weight × grade) / 5.

Growth Modifier · Revenue YoY

Breakout (≥+20%) +10 · Strong (+10-20%) +7 · Stable (+3-10%) +4 · Flat (0-3%) 0 · Slight decline (−3-0%) −3 · Declining (<−3%) −7. Captures trajectory without restructuring the composite.

Final Grade · Composite + Modifier

Final score = composite + growth. Breakout YoY can lift a branch by a full letter; declining can drop one. Cover shows both halves so the BM can read landing AND trajectory at once.

We grow by growing our people and customers — not at their expense. Critical Numbers carry 30% combined weight in the composite — the quarterly theme isn't a footnote, it's a structural anchor. Growth lives alongside as a separate signal. NOI/Truck sits in the Financial Engine top block as ungraded reference this quarter (actively calibrating). Strategic Weight tier on the cover hero is a parallel display lens — measures cash contribution scale, separate from the operational composite.

NOI/Truck is the OPSP economic denominator — "profit per truck" is how we measure unit economics for the company. Methodology & Source Map

📍 How the grade is built
Branch grade = a 7-metric weighted composite plus a single growth-modifier adjustment. Each metric counts exactly once. Critical Numbers (Q1: Driver Safety + Personnel-Budget) preserved at 30% combined weight — the quarterly theme spine.

📍 Composite Math
SG=5, G=4, W=2 (Watch), R=1. Score = (Σ weight × grade) / 5. Houston composite = (20·2 TUR + 10·5 Sales + 15·5 NPS + 15·5 eNPS + 10·5 Recog + 15·2 DSS + 15·1 Personnel) / 5 = 335 / 5 = 67. Anchor points: all-Green = 80 (B+ baseline), all-Super Green = 100 (A+), all-Red = 20 (F floor).
📍 Growth Modifier Rubric
Breakout (≥+20%) +10 · Strong (+10-20%) +7 · Stable growth (+3-10%) +4 · Flat (0-3%) 0 · Slight decline (−3-0%) −3 · Declining (<−3%) −7. Houston's −5.6% YoY → Declining → −7. Final 67 + (−7) = 60 → C.
📍 Letter thresholds
95+ A+ · 90+ A · 85+ A− · 80+ B+ · 75+ B · 70+ B− · 60+ C · 50+ D · <50 F.
